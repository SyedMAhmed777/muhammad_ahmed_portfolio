01 — Overview

Therapy, tracking and
community in one place.

Most mental-health apps do one thing well: meditation, mood journaling, or therapist matching. Serene was designed as a single, calm home for all of it — so a person in a difficult moment doesn't have to juggle five apps to get the help they actually need.

The product spans two connected experiences: a client app for booking, chatting, and self-monitoring, and a therapist dashboard for managing appointments and consultations.

02 — The Problem

Asking for help is hard.
The tools shouldn't be.

Through interviews with prospective users and a competitive teardown of leading platforms, four friction points kept surfacing — each one a moment where a person almost gave up.

01

Discovery is opaque

Users can't easily compare therapists by specialty, price, or reviews — picking one feels like guesswork.

02

Booking is bureaucratic

Calendars, intake forms, and payment portals live in different places, killing momentum mid-funnel.

03

Tracking lives elsewhere

Sleep, mood and activity data sits in separate apps the therapist never sees, so context gets lost.

04

Self-help is shallow

Free resources are scattered across YouTube and blogs. There's no curated library tied to a real plan.

“

“I downloaded three different apps before my first session — one to find a therapist, one to book, one to track how I was feeling. By the time I was ready to talk, I was already exhausted.”

— User interview, 28, first-time therapy seeker

03 — Goals

Design principles
that guided every screen.

Reduce time-to-first-session to under 5 minutes

From opening the app to a confirmed booking — without losing the user to a form.

Make wellness data feel supportive, not surveilling

Use soft color, gentle illustrations and human numbers — never red alerts or shame loops.

Create one shared context between client and therapist

The therapist sees what's relevant — sleep, mood trends — without the client typing a recap each session.

Build a free, curated path for those who aren't ready for therapy yet

A library, blog and group chats give value before any payment is asked.

07 — Key Screens

Every surface, organized
by what the user is trying to do.

7.1 — Onboarding

A welcoming first 30 seconds.

Branded but minimal. Social options, three required fields, and a single forgivable mistake state.

Login

Email + password with social SSO. "Remember me" and password recovery sit inline so users don't dig.

Create account

Four fields max — name, email, password, age. Age is used to surface relevant therapists later.

7.2 — Client Home

A daily glance, not a homework assignment.

Today's date, sleep, mood, activity and nutrition all on one scroll — capped with a suggested therapist for momentum.

Top-of-screen wellness summary

Sleep and mood are paired because they're the two most predictive signals of how the day will feel.

A single ring for nutrition

Macros are split inside one ring instead of four — less cognitive load, more "am I on track?"

Suggested Therapist card

Closes the loop: tracking is great, but if you'd like to talk to someone, here's a path that takes one tap.

7.3 — Discovery

Comparing therapists like you'd compare anything else.

Specialty pill, social proof (clients · ratings), price-per-hour and a single "View Profile" — all visible without tapping.

Search

Result cards prioritize the three trust signals: who, what, how much.

Therapist profile

About, library of intro videos, real reviews, and a sticky "Book Appointment" footer that always stays in reach.

Client profile (therapist view)

When the therapist opens a client, sleep, mood and calorie trends are the first things they see.

7.4 — Booking & Payment

From "Book" to "Confirmed" in two taps.

A horizontal date picker, time-slot grid, and saved payment methods — no typing required.

Book Appointment

Friendly illustration, swipeable dates, and time slots in a relaxed grid — booking shouldn't feel like an airline form.

Payment

Saved methods first, "Add new" last. The Confirm CTA stays calmly disabled until a method is selected.

7.5 — Sessions & Conversations

The room where it happens.

Calls, 1:1 chats, group support rooms, and a session history — every modality lives behind one familiar layout.

Consultations

Upcoming sessions with a one-tap rejoin & cancel.

Video call

Full-bleed video, picture-in-picture, classic three-button control.

1:1 chat

Date dividers, asymmetric bubbles, voice + photo input baked in.

Group support

Topic-based rooms (Anxiety, Depression…) for moments between sessions.

Session history

Past sessions stay accessible with a recorded clip and quick-delete — useful for therapists revisiting context.

7.6 — Self-care monitors

Numbers that feel like a friend asking how you slept.

Each monitor follows the same template: a calendar strip, a hero ring, and a 7-day stat — so the cognitive cost stays at zero.

Mood Monitor

Five emoji states, weekly stacked bars — never a "score".

Sleep Monitor

Bed time + wake time with REM / Deep / Light split.

Steps Counter

Distance, time and calories on one card.

Nutrition

Macros split inside the energy ring.

7.7 — Resources

A free path for those not ready to book — yet.

The library is a quiet, generous on-ramp. No paywalls, no upsells — just curated videos, a blog, and group chats by topic.

Library — topics

Anxiety, Depression, Stress, Loneliness, ADHD, OCD, Divorce — labelled in plain language.

Library — detail

Editorial cover, "Group Chat" entry point, and a searchable feed of bite-sized videos.

Blog

Long-form articles for the people who'd rather read than watch.

7.8 — Therapist Dashboard

A working day, in one screen.

Two big stat tiles set the tone for the day. Below, every upcoming appointment is a single tap from a video call or a respectful cancel.

321
Total appointments handled to date — quietly motivating without being a leaderboard.
5
Upcoming today — bounded so the day feels finite, not endless.
Two-button row on every appointment

Yellow camera = "join now". Red ✕ = "we need to reschedule." No ambiguous icons, no nested menus.

7.9 — Premium

Monetization that doesn't beg.

A single, comparison-led upsell screen. Three plans, a transparent feature matrix, and a cancellation note in plain English at the bottom.

Free vs. Pro feature matrix

Checks and dashes — no FOMO copy, no "limited time" countdowns.

Yearly is pre-selected

Best value for the user, healthiest unit economics for the business — both wins are honest.

"Subscription will auto-renew. Cancel anytime."

The footnote that builds long-term trust — surfaced at the moment of decision.

08 — Outcomes

What changed once
the screens were on devices.

Tested with 8 participants in moderated sessions — 6 prospective clients, 2 therapists. Tasks included finding a therapist, booking a session, and logging a mood entry. The patterns below held across the cohort.

3:42
Median time to a confirmed first booking — beating the 5-minute target.
100%
Of test users completed the booking flow without help on the first attempt.
4.6/5
Average "feels calming" rating from a 1–5 brand-perception question after the session.
What worked
  • · Sage-led palette consistently rated as "calming" and "trustworthy" in unprompted feedback.
  • · The unified monitor template (calendar → ring → weekly bars) made every health surface feel learnable in seconds.
  • · The sticky "Book Appointment" footer on profiles drove the highest task-completion rate.
What I'd change next
  • · Add a guided "first-time" onboarding that explains the monitors before they fill with data.
  • · Introduce filters on the search screen — specialty, language, price range — surfaced as chips above results.
  • · Build an empty state for the home dashboard that gracefully handles users with no tracked data yet.
  • · Explore an in-session note-capture flow for therapists, paired with a private summary they can share back.
